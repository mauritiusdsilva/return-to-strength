Welcome back to training

Helpful info

How to approach each session

Effort

  • Choose weights that feel manageable
  • Stop each set before it becomes a struggle
  • Focus on control, not lifting heavy

Rest

  • Rest around 60 to 90 seconds between sets
  • Take a little longer if needed

Session length

  • Weights: around 45 to 55 minutes
  • Cardio: around 20 to 35 minutes
When to increase the weight
If the target is 3 sets of 10 to 12
10·10·10 Keep the same weight next time
11·11·10 Keep the same weight and try to improve
12·12·12 Increase the weight slightly, with good control

Focus on smooth form first. More weight comes later.

Optional core work

Two lifting days include one short optional core exercise:

  • Monday · Pallof Press
  • Wednesday · Bird Dog
  • Friday · No core work

For now, skip crunches, sit-ups, and hard ab workouts. The goal is to rebuild control gently.

When to stop or scale back

Stop, slow down, or reduce the exercise if you notice any of these:

  • Pelvic heaviness or pressure
  • Urine leakage
  • Bulging through the tummy area
  • Pulling or discomfort around the C-section scar
  • Pain that stays or gets worse after the workout

If any of these keep happening, speak to a women's health physiotherapist before pushing further.

Exercises we are skipping for now

For the first 6 weeks, this plan avoids direct leg workouts such as:

  • Squats
  • Lunges
  • Leg press
  • Romanian deadlifts
  • Step-ups

These can be added later once training feels comfortable and consistent.

After 6 weeks

After 6 weeks, review how training feels. If the workouts and cardio sessions feel comfortable, the next phase can slowly add light leg strengthening.

This plan was made for a 33-year-old nursing mother, 5 months after a C-section, who has been cleared by her doctor to return to training.
